## 12. Approved vs. Risky Language (FDA/FTC Compliant Word List — CRITICAL)

**Why this matters (May 2026 context):**
BPC-157, TB-500, MOTS-c and similar peptides are **not FDA-approved** for human use. They are under formal Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee review (July 23-24, 2026). Any language that implies they "cure," "heal," or "treat" disease/conditions can be interpreted as an unapproved drug claim → FTC/FDA enforcement risk + Arizona Board of Nursing scope violation.

**HIGH-RISK WORDS — NEVER USE (Instant FAIL on checklist — rewrite immediately):**
- cure, cures, curing, cured
- heal, heals, healing, healed, "heal faster", "heal injuries", "promote healing"
- treat, treats, treating, treatment (when referring to any condition or person)
- prevent, prevents, prevention (disease or condition)
- diagnose, diagnosis
- fix, eliminate, reverse, eradicate, destroy (symptoms, conditions, disease)
- miracle, breakthrough, game-changer, guaranteed, "works every time", 100% effective

**CRITICAL — Word Boundary Rule:**
These checks apply to WHOLE WORDS only — NOT substrings inside other words. Specifically:
- "**health**", "**healthy**", "**healthcare**" are SAFE — they do NOT contain the forbidden word "heal"
- "**NurseRobHealth**" (X handle), "**nurserobhealth.com**" (website), "**nurse@nurserobhealth.com**" (email) are ALWAYS SAFE
- "heal" is only a violation when it stands alone or in forms like "healing" / "healed" / "heals" — never when part of "health"
- Same logic for all other words: "cure" ≠ "secure" or "procure"; "fix" ≠ "prefix" or "suffix"
- **If in doubt about a substring match → it's a false positive. Only flag the standalone word.**

**MEDIUM-RISK — ONLY in these exact safe phrasings + full disclaimer:**
- repair → ONLY "support the body's natural repair processes" OR "research on tissue repair mechanisms in the literature"
- support → "support healthy cellular function" or "support general wellness"
- promote → "promote general wellness discussions" or "promote natural processes"
- aid / help → "may aid general recovery conversations in research"

**SAFE / PREFERRED LANGUAGE (Use these instead):**
- "general educational information on how these compounds are typically discussed in the literature"
- "support healthy function / natural cellular processes"
- "practical considerations for people exploring these topics"
- "research interest in..."
- "the body's own mechanisms"
- "general wellness and recovery discussions"

**Rule for Hermes:**
If the sentence could be read by a regulator or Board investigator as claiming a medical benefit or outcome for a real person → rewrite using only SAFE language or remove the sentence entirely.
