# Arizona EV Charging & HOA Statutes
## Legal Research — May 2026

---

## Critical Finding: Arizona Has NO EV Right-to-Charge Law

Arizona is **not** on Plug In America's list of Right-to-Charge states (those are: CA, CO, CT, IL, OR, D.C. — which cover both owners AND renters). There is no Arizona statute that explicitly grants homeowners the right to install EV charging stations against HOA restrictions.

This means HOA proposals must rely on broader property-rights statutes and precedent.

---

## Usable Statutes

### ARS §33-1817(B)(3) — Architectural Review Standard ⭐ PRIMARY

> *"Approval of a construction project's architectural designs, plans and amendments shall not unreasonably be withheld."*

Full text at: https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01817.htm

**How to use it:** This is the direct standard governing ACC review. The committee cannot deny a project on arbitrary, capricious, or unspecified grounds. A denial must cite a specific, reasonable CC&R provision that the project violates. A flush-mounted charging pad that looks like ordinary concrete, installed on private property with no visual impact from the street, has no reasonable grounds for denial.

Section 33-1817 also establishes procedural protections:
- (B)(1): Design review committee must include at least one board member as chair
- (B)(2): Deposit/trust account requirements for construction projects
- (B)(3): The "shall not unreasonably be withheld" mandate

---

### ARS §33-1816 — Solar Energy Devices ⭐ PRECEDENT

> *"Notwithstanding any provision in the community documents, an association shall not prohibit the installation or use of a solar energy device... An association may adopt reasonable rules regarding the placement of a solar energy device if those rules do not prevent the installation, impair the functioning of the device or restrict its use or adversely affect the cost or efficiency of the device."*

Full text at: https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01816.htm

**How to use it:** While this statute specifically protects solar, it establishes a clear Arizona public policy: HOAs may not unreasonably block residential energy infrastructure on private property. The charging pad serves the same fundamental purpose as solar panels — enabling clean, cost-effective home energy use. Argue by analogy.

Key language to quote in proposals: "shall not prohibit the installation" and "reasonable rules... if those rules do not prevent the installation, impair the functioning... or adversely affect the cost or efficiency."

The statute also provides: "the court shall award reasonable attorney fees and costs to any party who substantially prevails" — this is a deterrent against arbitrary HOA denial.

## WRONG STATUTE — DO NOT CITE

### ARS §33-1818 — Community Authority Over Public Roadways ❌

This statute governs whether HOAs can regulate public streets after declarant control ends. It has NOTHING to do with EV charging, solar, or energy devices. A previous contingency plan incorrectly cited this as an "EV charger rights" statute. It is not.

Full text at: https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01818.htm

## Additional Context

### SB1501 (2023) — Utility Rate Regulation

This bill (ARS §40-214) regulates how UTILITIES price EV charging — it prevents utilities from using ratepayer funds to build commercial charging stations. It does NOT address HOA/homeowner rights. Not useful for ACC proposals.

### Arizona HOA Legal Landscape

- Arizona Planned Community Act: Title 33, Chapter 16 (ARS §§33-1801 through 33-1818)
- Key sections for homeowners: 33-1808 (open meetings), 33-1816 (solar), 33-1817 (architectural review)
- Sonora Parke HOA managed by Gerson Realty (Mesa, AZ 85207)
- No Arizona case law specifically addresses HOA denial of residential EV charging (as of 2026)

### Federal Support

- Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (P.L. 117-58): $7.5B for EV charging infrastructure
- DOE identifies home charging as the preferred EV adoption method (>80% of charging occurs at home)
- These are persuasive but not binding on HOAs — use as supporting context, not as legal authority
