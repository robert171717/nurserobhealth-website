# Healthy Alfred Visual Strategy — Competitive Intel

**Source:** X API analysis of @HealthyAlfred (14.3K followers, 855 media), May 2026.

## Thread Image Pattern

Healthy Alfred places images on the **evidence tweet** — not the hook tweet, not the education tweet. 

From his BPC-157 eye thread (ID 2055328675743097039):

| Position | Content | Images | Bookmarks |
|----------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Tweet 1 | "a stomach peptide healed an EYE?" — hook | 0 | — |
| Tweet 2 | "what even is BPC-157?" — education | 0 | 5 |
| **Tweet 3** | "the eye study isn't even the craziest part" — evidence bomb | **4 screenshots** | **12** ← highest |
| Tweet 4 | "my doctor said dry eye is permanent" — specific example | 1 screenshot | 3 |
| Tweet 5 | "LASIK gave me permanent dry eye" — another example | 0 | 5 |
| Tweet 6 | "studies:" — PMID citation dump | 0 | 4 |

**Key insight:** Images at Tweet 3 (the evidence peak) got the most bookmarks. The hook and education tweets are text-only. The images are raw PubMed abstract screenshots — portrait orientation, 1179×1533px, not custom graphics.

## Image Types

All 5 images across the thread are **photo** type — actual screenshots of research papers, not generated graphics. This is a deliberate credibility signal: "I screenshotted this from the actual journal."

## Application to Nurse Rob Threads

- **Image placement:** Tweet 3 (research evidence tweet), not Tweet 1 (hook)
- **Image count:** 1-2 images on the evidence tweet (stat card + PubMed screenshot)
- **Image type:** PubMed abstract screenshots preferred over custom graphics for credibility
- **Stat card:** Optional supplement — custom brand graphic that summarizes key findings
- **Never:** Place images on the hook tweet (distracts from the scroll-stop moment)
