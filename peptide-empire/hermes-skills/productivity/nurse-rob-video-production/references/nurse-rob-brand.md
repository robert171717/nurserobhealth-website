## Colors

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Primary Navy | `#1B2A4A` | Headlines, backgrounds, primary text |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, light backgrounds |
| Medical Red | `#C0392B` | Accents, CTAs, badges, lines |
| Light Gray | `#F7F9FC` | Page background, breathing room |
| Muted Text | `#5D6D7E` | Body copy, subtitles, secondary info |
| Dark Navy | `#2C3E50` | Protocol text, detail elements |

## Typography

| Role | Font | Weight |
|------|------|--------|
| Headlines / Titles | Playfair Display | 700–900 |
| Body / UI / Captions | Inter | 400–600 |

## Photography Style

- Professional male nurse (30s), clean modern clinic background
- Navy scrubs, confident approachable expression
- "Nurse Rob, RN" subtle corner branding
- 16:9 landscape, 4K photorealistic

## Design Rules

- **Clean medical aesthetic** — no grunge, no flashy gradients
- **White cards on light gray** with subtle box-shadow (`0 4px 24px rgba(27,42,74,0.06)`)
- **Red used sparingly** — only accents, CTAs, and key emphasis. Never as background fill
- **Navy is the anchor** — headlines, primary elements, protocol details
- **"Nurse Rob, RN" always present** — branding bar or corner badge

## What NOT to Do

- No neon/fluorescent colors
- No dark mode (always light canvas)
- No generic tech fonts (avoid Roboto, system-ui as display)
- No stock-photo clinic imagery with random doctors
- No AI-generated faces that look generic — use real Nurse Rob photos or abstract medical graphics
- No red backgrounds — red is accent only
