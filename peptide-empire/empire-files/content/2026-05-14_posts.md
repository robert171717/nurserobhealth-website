🧵 As a licensed RN, most people think TB-500 is just "healing." The actual mechanism is fascinating — and way more nuanced than you've been told.

1/ TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) is an actin-binding protein. By sequestering G-actin monomers, it regulates cytoskeletal dynamics — the actual science behind enhanced cell migration, angiogenesis, and tissue repair.

2/ A 2012 study in the FASEB Journal found TB-4 improved dermal wound healing in diabetic models by ~40% via accelerated re-epithelialization. A 2015 review in Expert Opinion on Biological Therapy confirmed roles in corneal repair and post-MI cardiac protection.

3/ Research protocols typically use 2.5-5mg twice weekly for 4-6 weeks, targeting soft tissue — tendons, ligaments, and muscle. These are research protocols, not medical dosing. Always consult your physician.

4/ TB-500 is often stacked with BPC-157 since BPC targets localized GI/tissue healing while TB-500 works systemically. But this pairing has ZERO human safety data — it's theoretical synergy from animal models. Caution warranted.

5/ Safety flag from a nurse: TB-500 promotes angiogenesis and cell migration. In theory, this could feed undetected infections or dormant cancer cells. Anyone with active malignancy should avoid based on this mechanism concern.
