5/ Stacking context: If these peptides return to Category 1, compounding pharmacies can legally produce them again under 503A. That means standardized dosing, quality controls, and physician oversight — exactly what responsible use requires.

6/ Safety: What I check for as a nurse — compounding quality varies wildly. FDA oversight means batch testing, sterility validation, and potency verification. Without it, you're gambling. The July meeting could change that.

7/ Bottom line: The FDA's July decision will reshape peptide access in America. Stay informed. Stay safe. Don't inject anything you can't verify.
