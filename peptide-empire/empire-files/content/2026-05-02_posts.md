## Post 1: Case Study Thread — "What a Torn Rotator Cuff Taught Me About Peptide Protocols"
**Time:** 8:00 AM MST | **Format:** Thread (7 tweets) | **CTA:** Save + Follow

--- TWEET 1 (standalone opener) ---
🧵 I watched a 47-year-old construction foreman avoid rotator cuff surgery. Here's the protocol his sports med doc used — and what I learned as the RN monitoring his progress.

--- REPLY 1 ---
1/ As a licensed RN, I've seen enough shoulder rehab to know this: most people rush the healing and skip the foundation. This patient didn't. Let me break down what actually worked.

--- REPLY 2 ---
2/ The injury: partial supraspinatus tear, confirmed on MRI. Orthopedic surgeon said "surgery in 6 weeks if no improvement." Patient asked about alternatives. Doc suggested a structured rehab + peptide protocol. That's where I came in.

--- REPLY 3 ---
3/ The protocol had three phases. Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Absolute rest, ice, NSAIDs as needed. BPC-157 at 350mcg twice daily — research on rodent models shows accelerated tendon-to-bone healing (Sikiric et al., multiple studies from Univ of Zagreb). Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Physical therapy began. TB-500 added at 2.5mg twice weekly — thymosin beta-4 is involved in actin regulation and cell migration during tissue repair.

--- REPLY 4 ---
4/ Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): Progressive loading. PT sessions 3x/week. Peptide protocol continued. By week 10, patient had full passive ROM. By week 12, pain-free active ROM at 85% of baseline. The surgeon canceled the procedure.

--- REPLY 5 ---
5/ What I tracked as his nurse — and what YOU should track too:
• Weekly ROM measurements (goniometer — simple tool, huge insight)
• Pain scores (0-10 scale, morning + evening)
• Sleep quality (shoulder pain destroys sleep architecture)
• Functional benchmarks (reaching overhead, lifting 5lb object)

--- REPLY 6 ---
6/ The key lesson: peptides don't replace rehab — they support it. This patient still did 36 PT sessions. He still iced. He still slept in a recliner for 6 weeks. BPC-157 and TB-500 likely accelerated the healing cascade, but the foundation was disciplined recovery.

--- REPLY 7 ---
7/ Bottom line: If you're dealing with a soft tissue injury, the peptide conversation should happen WITH your provider — not instead of them. The best protocols stack clinical supervision, structured PT, AND targeted peptide support.
