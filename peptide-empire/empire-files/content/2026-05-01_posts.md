## Post 1: Educational Thread — FDA Compounding Update
**Time:** 8:00 AM MST | **Format:** Thread (7 tweets) | **CTA:** Follow + Save

🧵 The FDA just dropped a major update on peptide compounding access — and most people missed it.

---

1/ As a licensed RN, I'm tracking this closely. The FDA published a Federal Register notice on April 16, 2026, announcing a Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) meeting for July 23-24. This directly impacts YOUR access to compounded peptides.

2/ What's at stake? The PCAC will discuss which bulk drug substances get added to the 503A Bulks List — the official list that determines what compounding pharmacies can legally prepare. Many peptides you use today are operating in a regulatory gray zone.

3/ The FDA's position, based on the April 2026 notice: They're specifically concerned about (1) peptide compounding that mirrors FDA-approved drugs, and (2) facilities compounding peptides without adequate quality controls. This isn't a crackdown on peptides — it's a crackdown on sloppy operations.

4/ Look, I'll keep it simple. If the July meeting results in stricter enforcement, here's what changes: compounding pharmacies will need to demonstrate batch testing, sterility validation, and proper sourcing. The ones already doing this? They'll be fine. The ones cutting corners? Their days are numbered.

5/ What this means for you as a patient or biohacker: the next 90 days are critical. If you rely on compounded peptides, now is the time to ask your pharmacy: "Do you provide third-party COAs? What's your sterility testing protocol?" If they can't answer, start looking elsewhere.

6/ As a nurse, I've seen both sides. I've watched patients recover from injuries faster with properly sourced BPC-157 and TB-500. But I've also seen contaminated vials cause serious infections — and those horror stories almost always trace back to pharmacies with no quality program.

7/ Bottom line: Peptide compounding isn't going away, but access IS changing. The compounders investing in quality, transparency, and clinical oversight will survive July's meeting. The rest won't. Choose your source like your health depends on it — because it does.

## Post 2: Hot Take — GLP-1 Reality Check
**Time:** 12:00 PM MST | **Format:** Engagement Post | **CTA:** Comment + Share Experience

Unpopular opinion: GLP-1 peptides aren't the answer for everyone — and the current hype is making people ignore the fundamentals.

Here's who the clinical data actually supports GLP-1 therapy for:

✅ Diagnosed metabolic dysfunction (HbA1c > 5.7)
✅ Obesity with comorbidities (BMI ≥ 30 + hypertension, dyslipidemia)
✅ Documented insulin resistance with failed lifestyle interventions

And here's who should think twice:

❌ Looking to drop 10 lbs for summer with no metabolic diagnosis
❌ No baseline labs, no provider relationship — just "biohacking"
❌ Personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer or pancreatitis

The APhA 2026 conference just highlighted this: GLP-1s are "rewriting the rules of metabolic disease." That's real. The cardiovascular risk reduction data from Wegovy's SELECT trial is genuinely impressive. New indications for sleep apnea are emerging.

But here's what the research actually shows: these drugs work best WITH lifestyle foundation — not as a replacement for it. Resistance training, protein intake, sleep quality — those still matter.

As a nurse, I've watched the pendulum swing from "GLP-1s are dangerous" to "GLP-1s are magic." Neither is true. They're powerful tools that shine brightest when the fundamentals are already dialed in.
