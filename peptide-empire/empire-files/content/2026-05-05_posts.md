## Post 1: Myth-Buster Thread — BPC-157 Oral vs Injectable: The Bioavailability Myth
**Time:** 8:00 AM MST | **Format:** Thread (7 tweets) | **CTA:** Follow + Save | **Pillar:** Myth Busting

🧵 Your BPC-157 capsule? It's probably not doing what you think it is.

---

1/ As a licensed RN, I get asked this constantly: "Can I just take BPC-157 orally instead of injecting?" The answer isn't as simple as Reddit makes it sound. Let me break down bioavailability the RIGHT way.

---

2/ What is BPC-157? It's a pentadecapeptide — 15 amino acids — derived from a protein in your gastric juice. Your body already makes a version of it. That fact is central to the oral vs. injectable debate.

---

3/ The research reveals the split: Most studies showing systemic healing (tendons, muscle, ligaments) used INJECTION. Oral studies exist — but they're almost entirely for GI conditions (IBD, ulcers, leaky gut). Different route, different target. Pharmacology 101.

---

4/ Why? BPC-157 is unusually stable in stomach acid — so oral CAN work for gut tissue. But systemic absorption into your bloodstream? Much less efficient. Research protocols for tendon/ligament healing typically use 250-500mcg injected near the site.

---

5/ Stacking reality check: Running BPC + TB-500 orally for a shoulder injury? TB-500 has near-zero oral bioavailability. You're likely flushing money. Injectable is the standard for systemic healing — full stop.

---

6/ Safety check: Oral BPC-157 is generally well-tolerated (mild nausea possible). Injectable? Sterile technique is non-negotiable — contaminated vials = infection. On blood thinners or history of GI issues? Doctor conversation first.

---

7/ Bottom line: Oral BPC-157 has a role — gut health. For tendons, ligaments, and muscle recovery? Research points to injection. Don't let marketing beat pharmacology. ⚠️ Education, not medical advice. —Nurse Rob, RN 🔗 Full protocol in bio.
