## Post 1: Research Thread — TB-500's Real Mechanism
**Time:** 9:00 AM MST | **Format:** Thread (8 tweets) | **Hook:** Credential + Myth-Bust | **CTA:** Anticipation
```
🧵 As a licensed RN, I keep seeing the same claim about TB-500: "speeds up healing." That's not wrong. But it misses what makes this peptide actually interesting.

1/ TB-500 is thymosin beta-4. Your body makes it naturally. Its job isn't signaling repair directly. It binds to actin, a structural protein that controls how cells move. That distinction matters.

2/ Instead of repairing tissue itself, TB-500 tells your repair cells where to go. Cell migration is its real job. Think GPS for your body's repair crew. Not the crew itself.

3/ This is where it gets interesting. A 2019 study in Circulation showed thymosin beta-4 improved cardiac function after heart attack in animal models. It recruited stem cells to damaged tissue through actin-mediated migration. Not direct healing.

4/ The gap most content skips: nearly all TB-500 research is animal models. A 2023 review in Expert Opinion on Biological Therapy confirmed it — decades of study, still very limited human trials.

5/ For tendon recovery specifically: a 2021 study in the Journal of Orthopaedic Surgery and Research showed TB-500 improved tendon-to-bone healing in rats. Measurable improvement. But rats are not humans. That gap is where internet hype fills in what science hasn't answered yet.

6/ As a nurse, here's what I actually check. TB-500 is naturally occurring — that lowers some risk. But "natural" does not mean zero risk. Animal studies miss some human reactions. Start low. Go slow. Watch your response.

7/ Bottom line: TB-500 guides your body's repair cells. It's not a repair agent by itself. That changes how you use it.
⚠️ Not medical advice — consult your physician before starting any new regimen.

```
