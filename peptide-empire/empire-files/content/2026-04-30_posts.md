## Post 1: Myth-Buster Thread — "The FDA Didn't Just Legalize Peptides"
**Time:** 8:00 AM MST | **Format:** Thread (7 tweets) | **CTA:** Follow for PCAC coverage

```
🧵 Everyone's celebrating that the FDA is "legalizing peptides." As a licensed RN, let me tell you what's actually happening — because the headlines are missing the fine print.
```

```
1/ First, what actually happened: On April 16, the FDA announced a Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) meeting for July 23-24 to review 7 peptides for potential inclusion on the 503A bulk substances list. BPC-157, TB-500, KPV, MOTS-c, Semax, Epitalon, and DSIP.
```

```
2/ Here's what nobody's telling you: The PCAC recommendation is NON-BINDING. The FDA doesn't have to follow it. And even if they agree, formal rulemaking requires a notice-and-comment period that typically takes OVER A YEAR. (Source: FDA Law Blog, April 21, 2026)
```

```
3/ Myth: "These peptides used to be legal and the FDA banned them."
Reality: These peptides were NEVER on Category 1. They were never legally permitted in 503A compounding. The 2023 move to Category 2 didn't ban them — it confirmed they were never authorized.
```

```
4/ That said — this IS a significant shift. HHS Secretary RFK Jr. has publicly stated he's "a big fan of peptides" (Joe Rogan podcast) and has prioritized reversing the 2023 policy. The political will behind this is real.
```

```
5/ The supply chain problem: Scott Brunner, CEO of the Alliance for Pharmacy Compounding, warned: "Flipping a regulatory switch without first ensuring the availability of pharmaceutical-grade active ingredients made under FDA manufacturing standards will create confusion, not clarity." (Reuters, April 15, 2026)
```

```
6/ What this means for you: If you're using peptides now, understand that current access remains unchanged. Nothing is legal today that wasn't legal yesterday. The July meeting is step one of a process that could take 12-18+ months before any real change.
```

```
7/ Bottom line: The FDA is finally having the right conversation — and that's genuinely good news. But "they're legalizing peptides" is misleading. We're looking at a long regulatory road with real supply chain hurdles ahead.

⚠️ Educational content only. Nurse Rob, RN provides education, not medical advice.

🔗 Follow for PCAC coverage as we get closer to July.
```
