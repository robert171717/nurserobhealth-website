### 🟡 4. FDA Clarifies GLP-1 Compounding Policies (April 1, 2026)
- **Date:** April 1, 2026
- **Source:** [FDA Drug Alert](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-alerts-and-statements/fda-clarifies-policies-compounders-national-glp-1-supply-begins-stabilize)
- **Action:** FDA reiterates that semaglutide and tirzepatide are NOT on the 503B bulks list or drug shortage list. Clarifies that compounding "essentially copy" products (including combos like semaglutide + B12) is impermissible unless the prescriber documents a "significant difference" for an individual patient, AND fewer than 4 prescriptions per month of that same product are filled.
- **Impact:** 🟡 IMPORTANT — The "safe harbor" of 4 or fewer prescriptions per month is now explicitly documented. This is the FDA's enforcement discretion threshold. Anything above 4/month is fair game for enforcement.
- **Content Angle:** "Did you know your compounder can legally fill up to 4 GLP-1 scripts a month? Here's the fine print." Lowkey important for audience education.
