What Is the Wolverine Stack?

The "Wolverine Stack" is the community name for combining BPC-157 and TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) — two peptides studied extensively in animal models for tissue repair, wound healing, and recovery. The name comes from the idea of Wolverine-like healing, but the reality is more nuanced.

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a pentadecapeptide derived from human gastric juice. Research shows it promotes angiogenesis, accelerates wound healing, and protects the gastrointestinal tract. It works locally — injected near the injury site or systemically for gut health.

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) is an actin-binding protein that regulates cytoskeletal dynamics. By sequestering G-actin monomers, it promotes cell migration, angiogenesis, and tissue regeneration systemically. Unlike BPC-157, TB-500 works throughout the body — making it complementary to BPC's localized action.

⚠️ Important: The stack has ZERO human safety data.

BPC-157 and TB-500 have never been studied together in human clinical trials. The rationale for combining them comes from animal research showing complementary mechanisms. Caution is warranted — especially regarding TB-500's angiogenic properties and theoretical cancer risk.

Dosing Protocols

BPC-157

  • Typical dose: 250-300mcg
  • Frequency: 2x daily (morning + evening)
  • Route: Subcutaneous injection
  • Cycle: 4-6 weeks on, 2 weeks off
  • Best for: Localized tissue repair, GI health, tendon/ligament recovery

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)

  • Typical dose: 2.5-5mg
  • Frequency: 2x weekly (e.g. Monday + Thursday)
  • Route: Subcutaneous injection
  • Cycle: 4-6 weeks on, 2 weeks off
  • Best for: Systemic healing, muscle recovery, cardiovascular protection

Mixing Math & Injection Guide

Both peptides come as lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder in vials. You reconstitute them with bacteriostatic water before injecting.

Quick Mixing Reference

BPC-157 (5mg vial)

Add 2mL bac water → 250mcg = 10 units on insulin syringe

Add 1mL bac water → 250mcg = 5 units on insulin syringe

BPC-157 (10mg vial)

Add 2mL bac water → 250mcg = 5 units on insulin syringe

Add 1mL bac water → 250mcg = 2.5 units on insulin syringe

TB-500 (10mg vial)

Add 1mL bac water → 2.5mg = 25 units on insulin syringe

Add 2mL bac water → 2.5mg = 50 units on insulin syringe

Injection sites: Subcutaneous injection into abdominal fat (2 inches from navel), rotating sites daily. Use alcohol prep pads. Never reuse needles. Store reconstituted peptides in the refrigerator.

Safety Considerations

🔬

Angiogenesis Risk

TB-500 promotes blood vessel growth. In theory, this could feed undetected tumors. Anyone with active or recent malignancy should avoid.

💉

Injection Risks

Infection, bruising, and injection site reactions are possible. Use sterile technique every time. Never share needles.

⚠️

Unknown Interactions

Stacking peptides creates unknown interactions. Even individually well-studied compounds may interact in unexpected ways when combined.
